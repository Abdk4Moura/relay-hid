# Relay — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-01_

Relay turns your Android phone into a keyboard, mouse/trackpad, and remote for your other
devices — over Bluetooth (acting as a standard Bluetooth keyboard/mouse) or over your local
Wi‑Fi network (to a companion "relay-desktop" receiver you run on your own computer).

**Relay does not have any servers, accounts, analytics, ads, or trackers. We never collect,
transmit, or store your personal data.** Everything happens on your own devices and your own
local network.

## What the app handles, and why

- **Keystrokes, mouse movements, and remote-control presses you make in the app** are sent
  directly to the device you are controlling — either over Bluetooth (to the paired host) or
  over your local network (to your own computer running the receiver). They are not stored or
  sent anywhere else.
- **Clipboard contents** are exchanged with your computer **only when you explicitly use the
  clipboard‑sync or "Share to Relay" feature**, and only over your local network to your own
  receiver. Relay does not read your clipboard in the background.
- **Files you choose to send** ("Share to Relay") are transferred over your local network to
  your own computer's Downloads folder. Relay does not upload them anywhere else.
- **Settings** (your chosen target‑device profile, key/trackpad preferences, snippets, the
  remembered Wi‑Fi host/port, and a locally‑generated pairing PIN) are stored only in the app's
  private storage on your phone.

## Permissions

- **Bluetooth (connect / advertise / scan)** — to appear as and connect to a Bluetooth
  keyboard/mouse. Relay does not scan for or track your location.
- **Local network / Internet** — used solely to reach your own computer's receiver on your LAN
  and to check GitHub for app/receiver updates. No personal data is sent in these requests.
- **Notifications** — to show a small, self‑dismissing status when a clipboard item or file is
  synced, and to keep the Bluetooth connection alive while in use.
- **Multicast/Wi‑Fi state** — to discover your computer's receiver on the local network (mDNS).

## Data sharing

None. Relay has no backend. The only network destinations are (a) devices on your own local
network that you connect to, and (b) GitHub, for update checks of the public open‑source
receiver. No data is sold or shared with third parties.

## Children's privacy

Relay collects no data from anyone, including children.
